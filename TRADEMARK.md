# Trademark policy

`ContentOps` and `SecM8` are trademarks of KustoKing / SecM8. This
policy describes how the marks may and may not be used.

The Apache 2.0 License under which the code is distributed
**explicitly does not grant trademark rights** (Apache 2.0 §6). This
document is the complementary policy that fills that gap.

## Quick summary

| You want to... | Allowed? |
|---|---|
| Fork the code, modify it, run it internally | Yes, no permission needed |
| Distribute your fork under a different name | Yes, no permission needed |
| Distribute a binary of unmodified upstream and call it "ContentOps" | Yes (factually accurate origin) |
| Sell support, hosting, or consulting around ContentOps | Yes, but see "Nominative use" below |
| Name your fork or competing product "ContentOps-Plus" or similar | **No** |
| Use the SecM8 logo or wordmark in your marketing | **No, without written permission** |
| Imply that SecM8 endorses, sponsors, or certifies your fork | **No** |
| Register a domain name containing "ContentOps" or "SecM8" | **No** |

## Nominative use

You may use the names "ContentOps" and "SecM8" in **factual,
descriptive** statements such as:

- "Our team uses ContentOps to manage our detection rules."
- "This module is compatible with ContentOps version X."
- "We provide consulting services for ContentOps deployments."

You may NOT use the names in ways that suggest **affiliation,
endorsement, or origin**, such as:

- A product or service name that includes "ContentOps" or "SecM8" as
  a substantial part of the brand.
- Logos or visual styling that imitates the SecM8 mark.
- A domain name where "contentops" or "secm8" is the second-level
  or otherwise distinguishing element (e.g., `contentops-enterprise.com`,
  `secm8services.io`).

When in doubt, ask in writing at the contact below.

## Forks

You are encouraged to fork the code. If you distribute your fork,
please:

1. Rename it — use a name that is meaningfully distinct from
   "ContentOps".
2. Remove or update the SecM8 wordmark in the README and CLI
   `--version` output. The Apache 2.0 License §4(b) already requires
   you to mark modified files; this is the corresponding requirement
   for the brand.
3. Keep the `LICENSE` and `NOTICE` files intact (§4(a) and §4(d) of
   the License).

## Commercial use

The Apache 2.0 License permits commercial use, including resale,
hosting, and integration into proprietary products. The trademark
policy does not change that. It only restricts the **name and brand**
under which you sell.

## Future tier upgrades

If commercial misuse becomes a real problem, the project may move
from Apache 2.0 to a stricter license (BUSL-1.1 or ELv2 are the
likely candidates). Any such change applies only to future versions;
versions released under Apache 2.0 remain under Apache 2.0 forever.
